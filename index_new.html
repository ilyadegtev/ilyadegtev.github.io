About me

I am a condensed matter physicist. I received my PhD in Physics from Sapienza University of Rome in 2025, where I investigated the structural and electronic properties of silver fluorides as analogues of cuprate superconductors. Prior to that, I obtained my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Physics from Lomonosov Moscow State University. My research combines scanning tunnelling microscopy (STM), X-ray spectroscopy (XAS and RIXS), surface science, and first-principles calculations to study quantum materials and strongly correlated electron systems. I am also interested in the design and construction of custom-made ultra-high-vacuum (UHV) equipment, thin-film synthesis, atomic-scale reactions on metallic surfaces, and spectroscopy of two-dimensional materials. I have experience in programming, data analysis, and modelling using Matlab and R. I hold two international patents and am a co-author of several scientific publications.

Research interests

  • Quantum materials
  • Strongly correlated electron systems
  • Scanning tunnelling microscopy (STM)
  • Surface science and atomic-scale reactions
  • X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS)
  • Resonant inelastic X-ray scattering (RIXS)
  • Polarized cryomagnetic Raman spectroscopy of 2D materials
  • Silver fluorides and cuprate analogues
  • Density Functional Theory (DFT)
